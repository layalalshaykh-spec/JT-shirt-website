01 · The Premise

A uniform program is infrastructure. Treat it that way.

Most companies still buy uniforms reactively. Sizes guessed, vendors switched, replacements ordered every few months. The cost compounds. So does the inconsistency. JT Shirts builds programs designed around how your crews actually work: standardized fits, durable fabrics, predictable reorder cycles, one consistent brand from foreman to field tech.

Frequent replacements

Low-grade fabric fails in months. Reorders pile up across the year, often at retail-tier pricing because the volume is broken into pieces.

Quality inconsistency

A new batch from a different vendor ships with off-shade fabric, wrong fit, missing logo placement. Crews look mismatched on customer sites.

Administrative overhead

Someone in HR or Ops tracks sizes by spreadsheet, chases vendors, fixes mistakes. The hours add up and the work never compounds.

Brand dilution

Faded logos. Mismatched colors across sites. Crews show up to a customer looking like contractors from three different companies.

03 · How We Run It

One program. Four phases. Built for repeatability.

No one-off purchase orders. No ad-hoc reorders. A standardized rhythm your team learns once.

Phase · 01

Audit

We map current spend, vendor count, replacement frequency, and brand inconsistency across your sites. Most audits surface 25 to 40 percent of cost hidden in fragmented procurement.

Phase · 02

Standardize

One spec sheet per role. Same fabric, same fit, same logo placement, every order, every crew. Quality controls enforced at production, not after delivery.

Phase · 03

Fulfill

Production runs on a predictable cycle. Lead times locked at 7 to 14 days. Direct ship to your sites or central warehouse. No middlemen.

Phase · 04

Monitor

Quarterly review of replacement rates, brand compliance, cost per crew. Data goes back into the spec. The program gets cheaper and tighter every cycle.
